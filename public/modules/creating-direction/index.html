This is gratitude.

For our first exercise we’re going to start with gratitude.

And contrary to popular belief, this exercise has almost nothing to do with being grateful.

It goes much deeper than that.

Before we get to that point however, what is gratitude?

Well, it’s important to understand that it can be absolutely anything you want it to be.

It can be a feeling. It can be a state of mind. It can be a simple thought.

There are millions of ways you can express gratitude.

Yet how you express gratitude is irrelevant.

What’s truly remarkable is the fact that you expressed something.

That you created some form of expression from your mind.

So what does this tell us?

For starters, it tells us that you have the ability to direct your mind.

That you’re not completely victim to your mental illness.

And that’s about it.

Basically, that you can direct your thoughts, opinions and judgements.

Of course, self-awareness is a skill.

Which means you must practice it in order to get better.

And these realisations can only make sense through practice.

So I want you to ask yourself on a daily basis:

What are you most grateful for today and how do you express it?

And when you’re doing it, I want you to take notice of how you express it.

Was it a thought? Perhaps a simple gesture? Via some form of self-respect?

Ask it when you have a spare moment to think, and really try and identify what took place.

Then once you feel comfortable expressing gratitude, you can move onto the next exercise.

Remember, one skill at a time.
