Introduction. Self-awareness

This is your brain.

Each line represents a thought, an opinion or a judgement.

Collectively they provide us with direction.

In order to make informed decisions.

(i.e. dance to your favourite 90s’ mixtape)

On the contrary, this is clarity.

Clarity allows us to single out our thoughts, opinions and judgements.

In order to evaluate them.

(so we can decide to keep them or let them go).

Clarity is important because it prevents direction from taking over.

As well as from disappearing completely.

Essentially…

A balance of clarity and direction is required to function sustainably.


Please don’t forget this.

Too little direction.

(and we may feel helpless, lost and confused).

Too much direction.

(and we are left irrational and obsessive).


And if we maintain these imbalances for too long?

Well, we can end up developing mental illness.


Which can make life incredibly difficult, if not entirely unbearable.

Of course, there are solutions.

(and this website only elaborates on one of them).

This solution is self-awareness.

Self-awareness is the process of being able to control your mind.

(intentionally, with purpose).

In order to regain a balance of both direction and clarity.

Self-awareness is a collection of skills that anyone can develop, to better understand how they create imbalance in their minds.
